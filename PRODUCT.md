# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Two partners in an Italian household who share responsibility for the family's recurring bureaucracy — bollo auto, revisione, RCA, TARI, PagoPA notices, ID renewals, utility bills, pediatric appointments, medicine schedules. Neither wants it to be one person's job, so the product is a shared source of truth both can trust. They use it in stolen moments, mostly on a phone: forwarding a voice note or photo to the bot between other tasks, or glancing at what's coming due. The job to be done is to stop carrying all of this in their heads and know that nothing recurring will slip.

## Product Purpose

FamilySherpa makes tracking family bureaucracy passive. A user forwards a voice note, a photo, or a PDF (via Telegram or in-app), the AI extracts what it is, when it's due, how much it costs, and which asset it belongs to, and the user taps Conferma and forgets — the app remembers, then reminds them via push and Telegram as things come due. Success is a household where nothing recurring is held in anyone's memory and nothing important is missed, felt as relief: the mental load is offloaded rather than merely organized.

## Positioning

The app that carries your family's mental load — capture-and-forget instead of track-and-manage. It turns the scattered, stressful admin of Italian family life into something passive, so the user never has to be the one who remembers.

## Brand Personality

Warm, human, and reassuring — it should feel like a caring helper looking after the family, not a piece of software. The Italian voice is gentle and personal, a quiet companion that never adds stress. Warmth here is emotional reassurance, not playfulness: the tone stays grown-up and dependable, matching the real weight of the responsibilities it holds.

## Anti-references

- **Cold government / bank portals** — the sterile, form-heavy PA and banking aesthetic FamilySherpa exists to rescue people from.
- **Childish "family app" clip-art** — cartoon mascots, pastel bubbles, toy-rounded UI that would trivialize real responsibilities. Warm must never tip into childish.
- **Loud SaaS dashboards** — gradient hero-metrics, glassmorphism, neon accents, "growth" energy; overstimulating for what should be a calm utility.
- **Cluttered legacy do-everything apps** — dense menus and feature soup where every screen competes for attention, the opposite of offloading mental load.

## Design Principles

Offload, never add — every screen should reduce what the user has to hold in mind; a new thing to manage is a failure, not a feature.

Warm without childish — reassurance comes from tone, copy, and calm, not from mascots or toy shapes; the interface stays grown-up because the stakes are real.

Capture, then disappear — the core loop is one-tap confirm and forget; minimize effort per item and get out of the way immediately after.

Foresight over history — surface what's coming (next deadlines, the cash-flow peak, the next dose) more prominently than logs of what already happened.

Reassure, don't alarm — reminders and warnings are the voice of a steady companion, never a bureaucratic or anxious one.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target is committed to, but accessible-by-default choices are the standard: readable contrast, keyboard-reachable controls, honoring reduced-motion. A CVD-safe categorical chart palette is already in place for the dashboard.
